Public Enemy Number One

During the 1930s Depression, many Americans, nearly helpless against forces they didnâ€™t understand, made heroes of outlaws who took what they wanted at gunpoint. Of all the lurid desperadoes, one man, John Herbert Dillinger, came to evoke this Gangster Era and stirred mass emotion to a degree rarely seen in this country.

Dillinger, whose name once dominated the headlines, was a notorious and vicious thief. From September 1933 until July 1934, he and his violent gang terrorized the Midwest, robbing banks and police arsenals, and staging 3 jail breaks.

Here at Dillinger Distillery, we attempt to carefully re-create that era, both it's beauty and ugly. We use the elements of our local history to push our bright future. Come sip on one of our finely crafted spirits today, and experience history in your own perspective.

About us

In 1997 John Doe, having a slight addiction to American History and a love for whiskey, dreamed of creating a place that could capture the taste of an era. With the help of his hard-working wife and a couple local investors, Dillinger Distillery was born later that year in July. John picked South Bend as home due to it's rich history with prohibition, and it being Dillinger's last robbery in his infamous career. A beautiful city which happens to be located on winding river, South Bend was home to industrial tycoons and brilliant scientists. Dllinger Distillery participates in many local parades and traditions.

To this day, John oversees all distilling operations as well as keeping up the storefront. Having served over 10,000 patrons as well as some notable celeberitys, Dillinger Distillery is still your best bet for a tasty and satisfying whiskey experience in Northern Indiana.
